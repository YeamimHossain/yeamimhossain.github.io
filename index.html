About

Biography

Md Yeamim Hossain earned his B.S. in Electronics and Telecommunication Engineering from the University of Liberal Arts Bangladesh in 2019. During his undergraduate studies, he developed strong academic and research interests in machine learning, data science, signal processing, biomedical signal analysis, and deep learning. His undergraduate thesis focused on the application of machine learning techniques to biomedical signal processing, working with signals such as EEG, and EMG for classification and analysis tasks, resulting in several publications in IEEE conferences.

He is currently pursuing an M.S. in Information Technology at Jahangirnagar University , where his coursework includes machine learning, artificial intelligence, data science, IoT, cloud computing, human-computer interaction, and data mining. He also served as a Teaching Assistant at ULAB, where he assisted in laboratory instruction, prepared course materials, and supported students through academic mentoring.

Beyond academics, Yeamim is passionate about research, project development, coding, and design. In his leisure time, he enjoys reading novels, watching films, playing video games, traveling, and spending quality time with family. He aspires to pursue advanced research and build a meaningful career in artificial intelligence and intelligent systems.

Thesis Work

M.S. Thesis · Ongoing
Robust Federated Learning under Distribution Shift in Biomedical Time-Series Data
Jahangirnagar University, IIT
April 2026 – Present
Supervisor: M. Shamim Kaiser, PhD

This research investigates the challenges of training federated learning models on biomedical time-series data — such as EEG, ECG, and EMG signals — where data distributions across multiple clients (hospitals, devices) vary significantly. The work proposes robust aggregation strategies and domain adaptation techniques to maintain model performance under heterogeneous, non-IID data conditions, with applications in decentralized healthcare AI and privacy-preserving diagnostics.

B.S. Thesis · Completed 2019
Detection of Motor Imagery Events in EEG Signals Using Artificial Intelligence Techniques
University of Liberal Arts Bangladesh
May 2019 – September 2019
Supervisor: Abul Barkat Mollah Sayeed Ud Doulah, PhD

This thesis explored the automated detection and classification of motor imagery (MI) events in electroencephalogram (EEG) signals using machine learning and artificial intelligence techniques. Feature extraction methods including spectral and time-frequency analysis were applied to EEG data, and multiple classification algorithms (SVM, ANN, k-NN) were evaluated for their performance in identifying distinct mental states. The research contributed to brain-computer interface (BCI) systems and led to two published IEEE conference papers.
